ABOUT THIS SITE

Rarely does the mayor of a city become a global celebrity. Mayor Rob Ford has received so much publicity and attention, it is easy to lose track of the issues that matter most to Torontonians. This website aims to provide nothing but facts, from an unbiased perspective, to help Toronto's voters form their own opinions about Rob Ford.

Ontario Divisional Court Justice Gladys Pardu ruled in favour of Rob Fords appeal to Ontario Superior Court Judge Charles Hackland verdict.


2012: Rob Ford found guilty in conflict of interest case

Ontario Superior Court Judge Charles Hackland issued a guilty verdict, that found Rob Ford guilty of breaching the conflict of interest regulations,Judge Huckland gave Rob Ford two weeks to vacate his seat on city council. Rob Ford commented that he would fight the verdict.


2012: Allegations of staff misuse

The Globe and Mail reported on that Ford may have used at least two city hall staff members to help run two summer football teams.


2012: Elimination of plastic bag fee*

The council led by Rob Ford, vote to eliminates the plastic bag fee, which was put in place as as part of an eco-program to help cut-down on the use of plastic bags.


2012: Application to remove Rob Ford from office

The application filed in 2012, dates back to the issue of the City of Toronto's Integrity Commissioner Janet Leiper ruling: Councillor Rob Ford violated City Council's Code of Conduct. Following the ruling, Ford then participated in a council debate to discuss dropping the case against him. Lawyer Clayton Ruby filed the application for removal under the charge that, Rob Ford's participation in the original debate, violated the Municipal Conflict of Interest Act.


2011: Private garbage*

2011: Jarvis bike-lanes*

Council supports Rob Ford's plan to remove the Jarvis bike lanes, at a cost of $270,000


2010: City Council votes to abolish vehicle registration tax*

City council votes on: putting a stop to Toronto's vehicle registration tax, and limit councillors' office budgets, the motions deliver on two of Mayor Rob Ford's election promises.


2010: Transit City plan - abandoned*

Rob Ford stated “Transit city is over, ladies and gentleman”, adding “the war on the car stops today.”. Rob Ford unilaterally canceled (overstepping his authority) the under-construction Transit City plan.


2010: Rob Ford becomes Mayor of Toronto

Wins support in 32 out of 44 Toronto wards.


2010: Rob Ford broke City Council's Code of Conduct

City of Toronto's Integrity Commissioner Janet Leiper ruled that Councillor Rob Ford had not followed City Council's Code of Conduct. The ruling confirmed that Ford abused his council position in his fund-raising efforts. The commissioner noted that donors had included lobbyists, clients of lobbyists, and a corporation which did business with the City of Toronto.
